These Are The Best Petite Jeans I Have Found

BY ALICE CARY
20 January 2023

It’s smug to say, but I think I’ve found the best petite jeans for women. Not just one pair, either – several styles of every silhouette, cut and wash. How have I lucked out, you may ask? I tried on 28 pairs of petite jeans, analysing the pros and cons, fit and finishings, of each on my 5ft 1” frame.

The key lesson I learnt: never try jeans on alone. You need a second opinion in order to land on a pair that you’ll love and cherish for years to come, otherwise you might settle for something mediocre – and nobody wants that.

Be it a friend or an expert – like the wonderful team at Selfridges’s expansive Denim Studio, or the sleek private shopping rooms at Matches Fashion’s Carlos Place – assistance is necessary. Selfridges’s denim aficionado Alfie educated me on the fact that silhouette is not the only thing to consider: grains, rivets and stitching are important, too. (Trust me, when you try on more than five pairs, you really start to notice the tiny differences.)

Another crucial learning: certain brands just don’t cater to petite women, and that’s fine, because there are plenty that have absolutely nailed the brief. Tailors also come in handy, so don’t be afraid to embrace alterations, rather than casting certain styles on the “no” pile. I also learnt to be open-minded about sizing, noting, for example, that boyfriend jeans are much better several sizes up, so that they sit on the hips rather than cinch the waist. Twenty-eight jean styles later, I’ve curated my edit of the best petite jeans for women out there. Enjoy! I’ve done the legwork for you, literally.

Sometimes, it pays to be simple. There were two particular styles that prompted me to jot down “great everyday jeans” in my notes: J Brand’s cult Teagan straight-leg high-rise stretch-denim jeans and AG’s straight-leg high-rise stretch-denim jeans. Both do exactly what they say on the tin, without any bells and whistles.

The best boyfriend jeans I’ve discovered are from Raey and Citizens of Humanity. But considering they’re my favourite style, I’d like to point you in the direction of some more brilliant boyfriend jeans, courtesy of Frame, below.

Former fit model Paige Adams-Geller knows a thing or two about denim. With years of experience under her belt, she launched her eponymous brand Paige, which specialises in timeless jeans styles. The Sarah slim-leg high-rise stretch-denim jeans caught my eye, not least because they’re super flattering, like a hug in denim form.
